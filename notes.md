## web 2008, eine Annaeherung

* dynamic collage: jerky and jagged
  * Links are points of intersection between one "page" and another. They interrupt the flow of reading and scanning
  * **Still true but more and increase in walled gardens**
* bricolage/recycling
  * Bricolage. Its association in French are to handymen and hardware stores, where it connotes improvisation and making do with the materials at hand.
  * **probably not true anymore (level of professionalization, every other medium moving to the web)**
* not sticky
  * Sticky media are ones where readers/viewers commit to one work and stay with it for extended periods until it is done, often because they have paid to experience the work. (movied videos books)
  * **Still true, even more so with more content (netflix, spotify, kindle), yet also trends like binge watching, reading analysis etc**
* viewed singly
  * Although there is nothing more public than what is posted on the Web, we are almost never a member of a group when we view the Web (except when it is projected).
  * **wrong. Status updates from audio/video apps, second-screen (twitter+TV), twitch, life-streaming (periscope etc).**
* self-paced and self-selected
  *  we constantly have to make decisions about what we are going to view next.
  * **sort of. Many commentators crying that soon facebook, netflix, spotify, maybe youtube channels, snapchat as PR platforms for teens**
* most annuals, few perennials
  *  People are surprised to find sites that are three or four years old and still working. Sites that are not maintained decay as their technology becomes obsolete and their links rot.
  * **unclear.**
